About the Lab

Where Agronomy Meets
Climate Intelligence

The Crops & System Simulation Lab (CSSL) at Ramakrishna Mission Vivekananda Educational and Research Institute (RKMVERI), Narendrapur, Kolkata is dedicated to advancing the science and practice of crop systems modelling, climate risk assessment, and sustainable agriculture—particularly for the vulnerable agroecosystems of coastal Bengal and the Indian Sundarbans.

Our work bridges the gap between cutting-edge simulation science and ground-level farming realities. By combining APSIM crop models, climate data analytics, and participatory field research, we develop evidence-based, actionable recommendations for smallholder farmers, policymakers, and extension agencies.

We are an active member of the global AgMIP-Rice Team and maintain a long-standing collaboration with CSIRO Australia (2016–present), reflecting our commitment to international, interdisciplinary science.

Research Areas

Our Core Research Themes

We pursue integrated, systems-level inquiry spanning simulation science, field agronomy, climate adaptation, and stakeholder engagement.

🌾

APSIM Crop Simulation Modelling

Expert-level development, calibration, and validation of APSIM models for rice, grain legumes (grass pea, lentil, mungbean), and potato in South Asian agroecosystems. First-ever development of the APSIM-Grass pea crop model.

🌦️

Climate Risk Assessment & Agro-meteorology

Quantifying climate variability and change impacts on crop production systems. Integrating seasonal climate forecasts and downscaled projections for farm-level decision support and risk minimization strategies.

🌊

Coastal Saline Agriculture

Systems research on rice-based cropping in the Ganges coastal zone and Indian Sundarbans. Developing salt-tolerant cropping sequences, water management strategies, and intensification pathways for marginal lands.

💡

Decision Support & Agricultural Advisories

Building and validating climate-informed decision trees, planting window advisories, and nutrient management guides. Translating simulation outputs into actionable extension tools for farmers and policymakers.

♻️

Carbon & Nitrogen Dynamics

Accounting for GHG emissions, carbon sequestration, and nitrogen cycling in paddy rice systems. Research on energy efficiency, nutrient use efficiency, and carbon credit potential in agricultural landscapes.

📡

Remote Sensing & Spatial Analysis

Applying multi-temporal SAR imagery (Sentinel-1) in Google Earth Engine for rice area monitoring and flood inundation mapping in the Sundarbans. Integrating spatial analytics with crop simulation for regional assessments.
